### Print Specifications (Labels)

- **Format:** PDF or EPS vector, CMYK
- **Resolution:** 300 DPI minimum if any raster elements — sun mark must be vector
- **Size:** Die-cut to shape — 1.75" × 2.25" wrap, 0.75" circle top
- **Laminate:** None, or matte soft-touch only — do not gloss. Must match uncoated card feel.
- **Fonts:** Embedded or outlined

**Label printing options:**
- Canva (acceptable for first run — import brand colors manually, download PDF)
- Avery label templates (fit standard Avery 22847 for 10mL bottles)
- StickerMule (preferred for higher quantity — custom die-cut, matte finish, CMYK upload)
- OnlineLables.com — LP4TB or compatible 10mL wrap template

## SUN MARK AT SMALL FORMAT

### Specification for Roll-On Labels

At 0.30" diameter on a 10mL bottle wrap, the full sun mark is too dense. Use the **8-ray simplified sun** at this scale.

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| Variant | 8-ray simplified sun |
| Diameter | 0.30" (7.62mm) on wrap label |
| Diameter | 0.20" (5.08mm) on top dot label |
| Fill | Gold Sun `#C8922A` — same as all other formats |
| Stroke | Rays solid-filled (no outline), approx. 0.008" stroke at this size |
| Center | Solid circle, 0.06" diameter (20% of total mark diameter) |
| Opacity | 100% — no watermark treatment at this scale |

**Construction note:** At sub-0.40" sizes, the full sun detail becomes mud. Eight rays evenly spaced read cleanly. The simplified mark is not a compromise — it is the correct tool for the format. Consistent with the mark usage table in the Brand Token Spec.

**Never at small format:**
- Outline-only sun on any ground
- White sun
- Sage or bark sun
- The full multi-ray mark at this size

## PRODUCTION NOTES FOR ANISSA

These formulas are starting points. They are drawn from common knowledge of these materials and their pairings — not from Anissa's existing practice.

Before selling:
1. Make a small test batch of each (2–5mL) with the suggested ratios
2. Use on yourself for at least 3 days
3. Adjust what does not feel right
4. Make one batch for someone you trust and ask for honest feedback
5. Finalize the formula, label it "Version 1 — [date]" in your records
6. Do not change a formula after people have bought it without noting the change

**Labeling compliance note:** Essential oil roll-ons are generally regulated as cosmetics, not drugs, if no therapeutic claims are made on the label. Avoid language like "treats," "cures," "relieves" — stick to descriptive and sensory language (which this copy already does). If Anissa intends to make health claims, consult with a local attorney before production.
