Practice Bio — Short (Third Person)
Anissa Russell is a Licensed Massage Therapist and Reiki Master Teacher with twelve years of practice in the Summerville and Charleston area. Her sessions blend therapeutic massage and energy work into a single integrated experience — a sanctuary where the physical and the energetic are met together, not separately. She works with people who are ready to receive something real.
About Statement (First Person)
I create a perfect environment for healing. What happens inside it is real, ancient, and yours.

I hold the space. I read what is present. I respond to what is actually there — in your body, in the room, in the session as it moves. I do not work from a script. I work from what I notice.
What to Expect (Second Person · Client-Facing)
When you come to Anissa, you do not need to know exactly what you need. You bring yourself — whatever version of yourself arrived today — and you are met there without judgment and without agenda.

What most people notice first is that time changes. The room becomes a kind of sanctuary — warm, unhurried, safe in a way that is hard to describe but immediately recognizable.
Referral Activator — Original
I have been thinking about the people I most love working with, and you came to mind.

If there is someone in your life who is carrying something heavy right now, I would be honored if you passed my name along.

No pitch needed. Just: there is someone who might be able to help.

Three Minor Notes (Non-Blocking)
Note 1 · Clear Breath Product Copy
"You will feel it within thirty seconds" tips toward promising an outcome. Recommended softening: "Most people feel it within thirty seconds." Non-blocking — factually accurate for eucalyptus, not a therapeutic claim.
Note 2 · HTML Variable Naming
Both HTML tools use flat variable names (--umber, --bark) rather than the semantic role layer (--text-primary, --surface-primary). Hex values are correct. No visual impact. A one-line comment in each CSS block would close this gap for any future developer.
Note 3 · Pricing Average Assumption
Gap math uses $125 as average blended rate assuming equal 60/90-min split. If Anissa's sessions skew 60-min, the truer average is ~$115. The directional conclusion holds regardless — the gap is real and large.
Delivery Order for the Anissa Conversation
  1. Brand Token Spec — tell her this is the foundation. Her designer and printer use it. She doesn't need to memorize it.
  2. Copy Library — walk through the voice. Ask which service descriptions feel most like her. Invite edits without framing them as corrections.
  3. Business Cards — show all three directions. She doesn't have to choose one. Recommend proofing before any full order.
  4. Archetype Quiz — explain it's for her, not clients. Ask her to fill it out and send it.
  5. Intake Form — demonstrate the flow. Show the thank you screen. Ask if the consent language feels right.
  6. Product Line — three roll-ons, here are the names, here is the copy, here are the starting formulas. The detailed spec is reference material.
  7. Pricing Snapshot — have the conversation before showing the document. Lead with: "What would it feel like to price the work, not just the time?"
