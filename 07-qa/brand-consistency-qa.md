## THREE MINOR NOTES (Non-Blocking)

### Note 1 — HTML Variable Naming Convention

**Deliverables:** archetype-quiz.html, client-intake-form.html

**Observation:** The HTML tools use a flat variable naming convention (--umber, --bark, --rose-blush) rather than the semantic role layer defined in the Brand Token Spec (--text-primary, --surface-primary, etc.).

**Why this exists:** The HTML tools are zero-dependency, self-contained files. Repeating the full semantic layer inside a single HTML file would add significant bulk without meaningful benefit — there is only one surface context per tool.

**Impact:** None on appearance or behavior. The hex values are correct. Any future developer picking up these files will find the variable names slightly mismatched from the spec, but the intent is clear from context.

**Recommendation:** Add a one-line comment to the CSS blocks noting that these are direct palette tokens, not semantic roles. Optional — low priority.

### Note 2 — Pricing Snapshot Average Rate Assumption

**Deliverable:** 06-internal-docs/pricing-snapshot.md

**Observation:** The gap math uses "$125 average blended" to calculate monthly gross. This figure is an average between $110 (60-min) and $145 (90-min) assuming a roughly equal split of session lengths. The assumption is not stated explicitly.

**Impact:** None on the document's purpose or conclusions. The math is sound if the assumption holds. Most practices skew toward 60-minute sessions — if that is Anissa's pattern, the actual average would be closer to $115 and the gap math should be adjusted accordingly.

**Recommendation:** If Anissa's session split is known, update the calculation to reflect it. The directional conclusion (significant gap between current and correct rates) holds regardless.

### Note 3 — Product Formula Disclaimer

**Deliverable:** 05-product-line/product-line-starter-kit.md

**Observation:** The blend formulas are noted as "starting points — Anissa to finalize." The caution line about citrus photosensitivity is included for AR-01, and the ylang ylang caution for AR-03. Peppermint/eucalyptus caution for AR-02 includes a note about children under 10.

**Completeness check:** All three products include an external-use-only statement and a "ask Anissa about sensitivities" prompt. The labeling compliance note at the end of the document covers the distinction between cosmetic and drug claims.

**Recommendation:** Anissa should review all caution lines with her own knowledge of these oils before printing labels. The notes here are drawn from standard essential oil safety practice — she may have additional considerations based on the specific suppliers or formulations she uses.

## DELIVERY CHECKLIST

When handing this package to Anissa, present in this order:

1. **Brand Token Spec** — explain this is the foundation everything else inherits from. She does not need to memorize it; she needs to know it exists and that her designer/printer should reference it.

2. **Copy Library** — walk her through the voice and the forbidden terms list. Ask her which service descriptions feel most like her. Invite edits without framing them as corrections.

3. **Business Cards** — present all three directions. Tell her she does not need to choose one. She can print all three or start with one. Recommend a proofing run before any full order.

4. **Archetype Quiz** — explain this is for her, not her clients. Ask her to fill it out and send it. What she writes there will inform everything going forward.

5. **Intake Form** — demonstrate the flow. Show her the thank you screen. Ask if the consent language feels right to her.

6. **Product Line** — do not hand her 30 pages of spec. Tell her: three roll-ons, here are the names, here is the copy, here are the suggested formulas. The detailed spec is reference material.

7. **Pricing Snapshot** — have a conversation before showing her this document. Lead with the question: "What would it feel like to price the work, not just the time?" Let her answer. Then show her the numbers.
