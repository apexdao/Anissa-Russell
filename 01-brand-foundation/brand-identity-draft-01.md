## Card Analysis: What the Current Cards Are Telling Us
### Card 1 Front — Blush Pink
**The Dotwork Hand**
Soft rose-pink linen ground. Pointillist anatomical hand — medically precise yet ethereal. Radiating sun in upper register. Vertical rotated serif text. Feels sacred, minimal, and quiet. The texture says hand-crafted. The imagery says healer, not technician.
> **Keep** — This energy
### Card 2 Front — Kraft Tan
**The Da Vinci Skeleton**
Warm kraft ground with da Vinci-style anatomical illustration, golden sun overlay, and geometric circle. More complex, layered, and intellectually rich. Deeper warmth. The gold feels premium. But the eye has no clear landing point — the information competes with the art.
> **Evolve** — The color, simplify the composition
### Card 1 Back — White Linen
**Scattered Suns / Clean Layout**
White ground, clusters of starburst suns scattered across the card, serif name and credentials. The scattered sun pattern is charming but energetically diffuse. Eye goes to the suns before the name. Text hierarchy is good but the scattered motif fragments attention rather than focusing it.
> **Evolve** — Consolidate the sun motif
### Card 2 Back — Kraft / Gold
**The Bold Sun Logo**
Single large centered gold sunburst as the primary mark. Name in caps, credentials stacked below. The most logo-forward version. Strong. The sunburst as THE mark is exactly right. But the text is dense and the line-weight hierarchy could be stronger. This is the closest to a true brand system.
> **Keep** — The sunburst as primary mark

## Three-Lens Analysis: Design + Energetic + Intentional
### I. The Design Lens
The cards have a strong instinctive aesthetic vocabulary — linen textures, warm tones, anatomical illustration, radiating sun. But they violate key design laws: no single focal point, no clear visual hierarchy, and text placed on busy illustration without enough contrast. The vertical text rotation on the front is evocative but makes it harder to read quickly at a networking moment. Two cards feel like two different brand identities, not one system.
> Consolidate into one system. One palette. One sun mark. One typeface pair. Let the texture and illustration support instead of compete.
### II. The Energetic Lens
Anissa voiced it herself: she wanted da Vinci's Adam because it's anatomical. She is drawn to the body as sacred geometry, not as clinical object. The sun motif keeps appearing in both cards — this is not accidental. It is her core symbol. She works with light, energy, and radiance as healing modalities. The blush pink feels tender and receptive. The gold feels generative. Both energies are real in her work and both belong.
> The sun is non-negotiable. It belongs on everything. The body-as-art imagery bridges physical and spiritual — keep that bridge.
### III. The Intentional Lens
From the transcript: she designs for who she wants, not the general public. She values excellence and humanity equally. She is not a turn-and-burn practitioner. Her ideal client feels her before they understand her. The scattered suns on Card 1 back send a scattered energy signal. The bold single sunburst on Card 2 back sends a centered, grounded, present signal. One of those matches who Anissa actually is.
> A centered, singular mark says: I know who I am. That is exactly the energetic message her practice needs to send.

## Archetype Discovery: Questions to Find Her Three Ideal Clients
*These questions are designed to help Anissa describe her clients without knowing she's describing them. The archetypes will emerge from her answers.*

> NOTE: These are questions to ask ANISSA — not a client-facing quiz. They are the source material for the Neurospicy Archetype Discovery Quiz (Step 4 in build plan), which is a separate client-facing HTML tool built from the archetype profiles her answers reveal.

### 01. Emotional State Mapping
*Think of the last client you saw where time disappeared and you felt totally in your element. Without saying their name — what was going on with them when they found you?*  
→ Unlocks the emotional/life-circumstance profile of her best-fit client.
### 02. Boundary Mapping
*If someone walked in and you immediately felt a little drained or guarded — what was it about them? Not who they were as a person, just the energy they brought in.*  
→ Defines the edges of her ideal client field by exclusion.
### 03. Need-State Sorting
*Do most of your favorite clients come to you for physical relief, energetic or emotional support, or do they not seem to know the difference — they just know something needs to shift?*  
→ Separates the three most likely archetypes: body-focused, spirit-focused, whole-person.
### 04. Organic Positioning
*When a client refers someone to you, what do they usually say about you? If you've heard it more than once, it belongs here.*  
→ Referral language reveals real positioning, not the one you think you have.
### 05. Service-Based Segmentation
*Are the people who seek out Reiki specifically different from your massage clients in any way you've noticed — mindset, life stage, what they're working through?*  
→ Surfaces potential distinct archetypes for massage vs. Reiki.
### 06. Lifestyle Context
*What do your clients tend to do for work or for their lives — not income bracket, but how do they spend their days and what do they care about outside of work?*  
→ Values and daily rhythms tell us more than job titles.
### 07. Brand Tone / Energy Field
*If your practice had a vibe — like a playlist, a room in a house, or a time of year — what would it be? Don't think too hard. First image that comes.*  
→ Bypasses analytical brain; felt-sense answer reveals aesthetic direction and client energy.
### 08. Pricing Psychology / Premium Tier
*When you think about charging your full rate without hesitation — what kind of client makes that feel easy? What do you know about them that makes it feel fair?*  
→ Directly defines the premium-tier client profile.

## Market Positioning: Southeast Corridor Research
### Where Your Rates Stand — and Where They Can Go
Your current rates are honest and underpriced — not by a little. The research confirms you are delivering a premium, multi-credentialed, integrative service at rates that belong to a newer practitioner. You have significant room to move up, stay well within local market norms, and still charge less than many comparable practices two hours away.
### Current vs. Market — Blended Session Pricing
| Service                     | Your Current Rate | Local Market (Summerville/N.Chas) Mid | Charleston Proper Mid | Where You Could Go        |
|-----------------------------|-------------------|---------------------------------------|------------------------|---------------------------|
| 60-min Blended Session      | $90 *(underpriced)* | $105–$115                           | $125–$140              | **$110–$120** *(still below local mid)* |
| 90-min Blended Session      | $115 *(most underpriced)* | $140–$150                         | $170–$185              | **$140–$150** *(just reaching local mid)* |
| 60-min Standalone Reiki     | $90               | $90–$100                              | $100–$115              | **$100–$110**              |
- **8 Years Experience** = Credential Premium of 40–100% over new practitioners  
- **Reiki Master Teacher** designation supports upper end of every range  
- **Collective setting** = lower overhead = you keep more at every price point
### Three Ways to Think About What These Numbers Mean
☕ **The Coffee Shop Comparison**  
A specialty coffee shop charges $8 for a latte. The gas station charges $2. Both are coffee. You are the specialty shop with a master roaster. You're currently charging $2.40. The people who want what you offer will pay $8 without blinking.
📚 **The Educator Analogy**  
A tutor with a master's degree and 8 years of results charges more than someone with a GED and 6 months experience. You have both the credentials (LMT + Reiki Master Teacher) and the track record. The market agrees. Your rate hasn't caught up yet.
🌿 **The Garden Metaphor**  
You've spent 8 years growing a garden that produces something rare. Charging entry-level prices for a rare thing doesn't make it more accessible — it makes you less sustainable. A thriving garden needs to sustain the gardener first.
🏛️ **The Geography Reality**  
Practitioners two hours away in Charlotte and Atlanta charge $160–$225 for what you offer at $90–$115. You are not a discount version of them. You are the same level of practitioner, building a practice in a market that will absolutely support $110–$150.
### The Suggested Path: Raise in Steps, Not All at Once
- Move the 60-min session to **$110** first.  
- Move the 90-min session to **$140**.  
- These are still below the local mid-market for your credential level.  
- In 6 months, reassess. The data shows clear room to reach $120–$130 (60 min) and $155–$165 (90 min) — still firmly within what the Charleston market considers fair.
### What This Changes: Your Wrists Will Thank You
At $140 for 90 minutes instead of $115, you can see two fewer clients per week and earn the same income. That is not a small thing. That is your hands staying healthy for another decade. Raising your rates is not greed — it is the business decision that keeps you in practice long enough to serve the people who need you.
